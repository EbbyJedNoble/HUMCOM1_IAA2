Rock Paper Scissors

Introduction

Rock Paper Scissors is a game that most of us have played since we were young. It is a very simple game that can be played by just using our hands. But nowadays with the existence of the internet, people can play against each other online. This wiki page will talk about what it is, how it is played, some strategies you can use to win the game, why it is played, and the history of the game.

How is it played?

The game is played by at least two people. Basing it on the name of the game, players have 3 weapons they can choose from: rock, paper, or scissors. Each of weapon wins and loses against a specific weapon. So essentially it is a luck-based game.

Mechanics of the game:
  • The player chooses what to play in hand from the 3 options which is rock, paper, or scissors.
    1. Rock beats scissors;
    2. Scissors beats paper; and
    3. Paper beats rock.
      • But it is a draw if both players played the same thing.
  • The goal of the game is to beat the opposing player/s
    • The winning condition is commonly decided by points which may be decided and agreed upon both players.
    • e.g. first to rack up 3 points wins, or 1 point if it is just a quick and instant game
What are strategies on how to win?
The game is luck-based since noone is able to read people's minds. But despite the game being decided by chance, there are few things that a player can do to possibly increase the chances of he/she winning. We can try to:
  • Predict the opponent's patterns by analyzing what he chose based on the previous games/rounds he/she has played;
  • Use bluffs to possibly confuse your opponent; or
  • Just play very random by not thinking which can make it hard for the opponent whose trying to predict your pattern.
Why is it played?

The game is very easy to understand and learn how to play. It is often played for entertainment or with situations where something has to be chosen.

History

The first known mention of the game was in the book of Wuzazu which was written by the Chinese Ming-Dynasty Xie Zhaozhi. It was stated in the book that the game dated back during the Chinese Han Dynasty (206 BC-220 BC). The game was called shoushiling at that time.

The earliest form of the modern version of Rock Paper Scissors was created in Japan in the late 19th century, and it was called Janken. Rock Paper Scissors had spread beyond Asia which was especially due to the increased Japanese contact with the west.

Up until now, the game is still very popular all over the world. It is said that its success was due to the appeal of its simplicity which can be understood by any audience especially among children.
